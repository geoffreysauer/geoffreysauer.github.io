Sonnet 106

William Shakespeare

When in the chronicles of wasted time
I see descriptions of the fairest wights,
And beauty making beautiful old rhyme
In praise of ladies dead and old knights,4

Then in blazon of sweet beauty's best,
Of hand, of lip, of foot, of brow,
I see their antique pen would have expressed
Even such a beauty as you master now.8

So all their praises are but prophecies
Of this our time, all you prefiguring;
And, for they looked but with divining eyes,
They had not skill enough your worth to sing.12

For we, which now behold these present days,
Have eyes to wonder, but lack tongues to praise.
